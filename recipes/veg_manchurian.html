Recipe of Veg Manchurian-

Vegetable Manchurian is the crown jewel of Indo-Chinese cuisine. It consists of small, savory "meatballs" made entirely from finely minced vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and beans. These balls are fried until crispy and then tossed in a glossy, "umami"-rich sauce made from soy, ginger, and garlic.

This "dry" version is specifically designed as an appetizer, where the sauce acts as a thick coating rather than a gravy. It’s salty, sweet, and spicy all at once, providing a complex flavor profile that pairs perfectly with the soft, melt-in-your-mouth texture of the vegetable balls.

Ingredients-

  • Veggie Balls: 2 cups grated cabbage, 1 cup grated carrots, 1/4 cup finely chopped beans.
  • Binder: 3 tablespoons cornstarch and 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour.
  • The Sauce: 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 1 tablespoon chili sauce, 1 tablespoon vinegar, and 1 teaspoon sugar.
  • Aromatics: 1 tablespoon finely minced garlic, 1 tablespoon minced ginger, and 1/2 cup chopped spring onions.
  • Veggie Add-ins: 1 small green bell pepper (diced).

Steps to make Veg Manchurian

  1. Drain the veggies: Squeeze the grated cabbage and carrots tightly to remove as much water as possible.
  2. Form the balls: Mix the veggies with cornstarch, flour, salt, and pepper. Form into small, bite-sized rounds. If the mixture is too wet, add another tablespoon of flour.
  3. Fry: Deep fry or air-fry the balls at 190°C until they are dark golden brown and crispy on the outside. Set aside.
  4. Sauté the sauce base: In a hot wok, sauté the garlic, ginger, and green chilies for a minute. Add the diced bell peppers and white parts of the spring onions, stir-frying on high heat.
  5. Thicken the base: Add the soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar, and sugar. Pour in a small "slurry" (1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water) and stir until the sauce becomes thick and translucent
  6. The Toss: Add the fried veggie balls into the wok. Toss quickly for 30 seconds so they are coated but don't lose their crunch.
  7. Garnish: Top with plenty of fresh green spring onions and serve immediately.
