Recipe of Gobi 65:

Cauliflower wings, inspired by the South Indian "Gobi 65" are the ultimate vegetarian answer to chicken wings. These bite-sized florets are marinated in a spicy, yogurt-based batter and deep-fried until they achieve a signature "crunch" that holds up even when tossed in seasoning.

The dish is famous for its bold tempered spices—curry leaves, mustard seeds, and dry red chilies. It’s an explosion of heat and tanginess that makes it impossible to eat just one. Whether served as a party starter or a crunchy side, it’s a guaranteed crowd-pleaser that proves vegetables can be just as indulgent as meat.

Ingredients-

  • Base: 1 large head of cauliflower (cut into bite-sized florets).
  • batter: 1/2 cup cornstarch, 1/4 cup rice flour (for extra crunch), and 2 tablespoons yogurt.
  • Spices: 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste, 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder, and 1/2 teaspoon turmeric.
  • The Tempring: 10–12 fresh curry leaves, 2 slit green chilies, and 1 teaspoon mustard seeds.
  • frying: Vegetable oil for deep frying.

Steps to make Gobi-65 -

  1. Blanch the cauliflower: Drop the florets into boiling salted water for 2–3 minutes. Drain and pat them completely dry—moisture is the enemy of crispiness!
  2. Make the batter: In a large bowl, whisk together the cornstarch, rice flour, spices, ginger-garlic paste, and yogurt into a thick, smooth paste.
  3. Coat: Toss the dried cauliflower florets into the batter until every nook and cranny is covered.
  4. Deep fry: Heat oil to 180°C. Drop the florets in batches and fry for 4–5 minutes until they turn a deep golden-red and feel light and crispy. Drain on a wire rack.
  5. Temper(The Secret Step): Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. Once they sizzle and pop, pour this aromatic oil over the fried cauliflower.
  6. Serve: Toss well and serve hot with a side of lemon wedges and onion rings.
