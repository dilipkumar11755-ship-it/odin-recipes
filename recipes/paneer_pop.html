Recipe of Paneer Popcorn:

Paneer popcorn is a modern vegetarian twist on the classic popcorn chicken. It features small cubes of Paneer (Indian cottage cheese) that are double-coated in a seasoned batter and panko breadcrumbs. This creates a thick, crunchy shell that protects the soft, creamy cheese inside.

These are the perfect "movie night" snack or party appetizer because they are easy to eat and pair well with almost any dipping sauce—from spicy sriracha to cooling mint chutney. The high protein content makes them surprisingly filling, and the contrast between the hot, squeaky cheese and the brittle crust is addictive.

Ingredients-

  • The Core: 250g Paneer (cut into 1/2-inch cubes).
  • The Coating: 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs or crushed cornflakes.
  • The Slurry: 3 tablespoons flour, 2 tablespoons cornstarch, 1 teaspoon oregano, and 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder.
  • Seasoning: 1/2 teaspoon paprika and salt to taste.
  • Frying: Neutral oil for frying.

Steps to make Paneer Popcorn-

  1. Season the paneer: Toss the paneer cubes with a little salt and paprika and let them sit for 5 minutes.
  2. Prepare the slurry: Mix the flour, cornstarch, oregano, and garlic powder with just enough water to make a medium-thick batter (it should coat the back of a spoon).
  3. Bread the cubes: Dip each paneer cube into the wet slurry, then drop it into the bowl of breadcrumbs. Press the crumbs on firmly to ensure a thick coating.
  4. Chill(Optional): Place the breaded cubes in the freezer for 10 minutes. This helps the breading stick better during frying.
  5. Fry until golden: Heat oil in a pan. Fry the cubes in small batches for about 2–3 minutes. They cook very quickly, so watch for that perfect golden-brown color.

    6. Drain: Place on a paper towel to remove excess oil.

  6. Serve: Dust with a little extra chili flakes or peri-peri seasoning and serve with a creamy garlic dip.
