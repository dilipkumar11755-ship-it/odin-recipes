Recipe of Momos:

Momos are a beloved dumpling tradition hailing from Tibet and Nepal, eventually becoming one of the most popular street foods across Southern Asia. These delicate parcels consist of a simple flour dough wrapped around a flavorful, savory filling, which is then steamed to perfection. The result is a soft, translucent skin that holds a burst of juicy, seasoned vegetables inside.

The magic of a great momo lies in the balance of textures—the slight chew of the wrapper paired with the crunch of finely minced cabbage and carrots. Often served with a fiery "momo achar" (tomato and chili dipping sauce), they make for a comforting snack or a light, healthy meal that is as fun to assemble as it is to eat.

Ingredients-

  • For the Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and water (as needed).
  • For the Filling: 2 cups finely shredded cabbage and 1 cup grated carrots.
  • Aromatics: 1 large onion (finely chopped), 2 cloves minced garlic, and 1 inch grated ginger.
  • Seasoning: 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, and salt to taste.
  • Fats: 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or melted butter.
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped spring onions or cilantro.

Steps to make Momos-

  1. Prepare the dough: Mix the flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead until you have a firm, smooth dough. Cover it and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling: Heat oil in a pan and sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic until fragrant. Add the cabbage and carrots, cooking on high heat for 2–3 minutes to remove excess moisture while keeping them crunchy.
  3. Season: Stir in the soy sauce, salt, pepper, and spring onions. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool completely.
  4. Roll the wrappers: Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls (about the size of a marble). Roll each ball into a thin circle about 3 inches in diameter, ensuring the edges are thinner than the center.
  5. Shape the momos: Place a spoonful of filling in the center of the wrapper. Lift the edges and start pleating them together towards the center, pinching to seal the dumpling into a pouch or half-moon shape.

    6. Steam: Grease a steamer basket with a little oil. Arrange the momos inside, leaving space between them to prevent sticking. Steam over boiling water for 10–12 minutes until the wrappers look translucent and are no longer sticky to the touch.

  6. Serve: Remove carefully and serve hot with a spicy tomato-sesame chutney.
